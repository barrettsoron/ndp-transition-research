The election of Avi Lewis as the new federal NDP leader generated mixed reactions across the party's Western Canadian base.

Lewis stated he would concentrate on "strengthening the grassroots before seeking elected office."

Alberta NDP Leader Naheed Nenshi said in a post on X that Lewis becoming the federal leader was "not in the interests of Alberta," writing that "Albertans deserve federal leaders who understand the importance of Alberta and our essential role in the federation."

Saskatchewan NDP Leader Carla Beck said policies and positions Lewis has supported would risk $13.6 billion in economic activity for Saskatchewan and stated she would not meet with him until Lewis reverses his stance on the province's resource sectors.

Manitoba Premier Wab Kinew and British Columbia NDP Leader David Eby both expressed readiness to collaborate with Lewis on energy initiatives. Ontario NDP Leader Marit Stiles, Nova Scotia NDP Leader Claudia Chender, Newfoundland and Labrador NDP Leader Jim Dinn, and Yukon NDP Leader Kate White also congratulated Lewis on his victory.

Conservative provincial premiers used the discord politically. Saskatchewan's Scott Moe claimed Beck was attempting to distance herself from Lewis's "radical policies," while Alberta's Danielle Smith similarly attacked the Alberta NDP's constitutional ties to the federal party.
