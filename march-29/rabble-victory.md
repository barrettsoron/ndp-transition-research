Avi Lewis secured a decisive victory in the NDP leadership contest held during the party's convention in Winnipeg, capturing 56 percent of votes cast through the ranked ballot system.

The final tally included nearly 71,000 votes, predominantly submitted online. Lewis received 39,734 votes, significantly outpacing his nearest competitor Edmonton-Strathcona MP Heather McPherson with 20,899 votes. Tanille Johnston placed third with 5,159 votes, Rob Ashton fourth with 4,193, and Tony McQuail fifth with 945.

In his victory address, Lewis criticized the Mark Carney Liberal government's policies, stating: "His moves do not add up to the vision Canadians truly want and deserve in this perilous moment."

Lewis outlined his platform priorities, which included public ownership alternatives for groceries, internet, phones, and housing. He promised "a 21st century electrical grid, an EV bus revolution and a heat-pump in every home built with Canadian steel."

Regarding taxation, Lewis declared: "It is time, far past time, to properly tax corporations and billionaires that have been riding a tidal wave of profits."

On international policy, Lewis emphasized the need for moral clarity, particularly regarding Gaza, stating support for "a government that acts with moral clarity when it matters" regarding the conflict.
