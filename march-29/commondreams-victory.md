Progressive activist Avi Lewis secured the NDP leadership position with 56% of the vote on Sunday, pledging to revitalize Canada's struggling left-wing party. Lewis, grandson of an NDP cofounder, defeated other candidates in the Winnipeg convention race.

The party has experienced significant membership losses to both Prime Minister Mark Carney's Liberals and Pierre Poilievre's Conservatives. Following devastating April elections under former leader Jagmeet Singh, the NDP dropped to just seven parliamentary seats — below the threshold for official party recognition.

Lewis's spouse, acclaimed author and activist Naomi Klein, introduced him at the convention, describing his victory as an invitation for Canadians to "dream big once again" and confront corporate greed as more than half the population struggles with basic necessities.

## Policy Platform

Lewis proposes ambitious "public options" addressing affordability through publicly owned grocery stores and banks competing against corporate monopolies. He advocates expanding Canada's universal healthcare system into comprehensive coverage including dental, pharmaceutical, vision, hearing, and mental health services.

Financing these initiatives requires implementing a wealth tax targeting Canada's richest 1%, who collectively own approximately $1.25 trillion — nearly matching the bottom 80% of citizens' combined wealth, according to Oxfam Canada research.

## Criticism of Government

During his acceptance speech, Lewis stated: "Banks made $70 billion in profits last year alone. Oil companies expect tens of billions more. Grocery executive Galen Weston alone possesses $20 billion."

He criticized Liberal tax cuts for wealthy Canadians, projected to cost nearly $76 billion over five years while eliminating approximately 57,000 public-sector positions by 2028.

Despite acknowledging Carney's current popularity, Lewis argued the PM's anti-Trump rhetoric masks deeper alignment with problematic policies: "Half a trillion dollars for weapons, slashing public services, sidelining indigenous rights, and enabling unregulated AI and pipelines."

## Electoral Challenge Ahead

Current polling shows the NDP at approximately 7% support, far behind leading parties. However, public opinion research indicates widespread support for NDP positions on wealth taxation and expanded healthcare across party affiliations.

Lewis concluded: "The NDP will start winning again because we will become that beacon to the 99%."
